## 1. Scope and how this policy applies

BitInt provides a blockchain intelligence platform and related services available at **bitint.io** (the **Service**).

This Privacy Policy explains how BitInt collects, uses, discloses, and protects personal data in connection with:

- visitors to the bitint.io marketing site,
- users of the authenticated Service (dashboards and APIs),
- communications with BitInt, such as support requests and commercial discussions,
- events, webinars, and similar B2B engagement activities.

Customer contractual documents, such as a data processing addendum (DPA), may apply in addition to this policy for Customer accounts.

## 2. Roles: controller vs processor

BitInt processes personal data in different roles depending on context.

### 2.1 When BitInt acts as a controller

BitInt acts as a controller where BitInt determines the purposes and means of processing, such as for:

- operating and securing the Service and website,
- administering accounts at the organization level,
- billing and subscription management,
- marketing site analytics and communications.

### 2.2 When BitInt acts as a processor

For many Customer accounts, BitInt processes **Customer Data** on behalf of Customer. In that context:

- Customer is typically the controller,
- BitInt is typically the processor,
- processing is governed by Customer instructions, the applicable DPA, and the relevant service agreement.

Authorized Users who want to exercise rights relating to Customer Data should usually contact the Customer organization that manages the account.

## 3. Information we collect

Personal data categories may differ depending on whether someone is a website visitor, an Authorized User, or a Customer administrator.

### 3.1 Website and marketing site data

BitInt may collect:

- device and browser data (such as browser type, language, approximate region, and device identifiers),
- log and usage data (such as pages visited, referrer URLs, date and time stamps, and error logs),
- cookie identifiers and similar tracking signals (subject to choices described in Section 11),
- contact form submissions and newsletter sign-ups (such as name, work email, company, and message contents).

### 3.2 Account and identity data

For the authenticated Service, BitInt may collect:

- name, work email, organization, and role,
- authentication identifiers (such as SSO identifiers, directory user IDs, and MFA status if enabled),
- account configuration data set by Customer administrators (such as team names, permissions, and feature toggles),
- customer support entitlements and plan indicators.

### 3.3 Service usage, audit, and API data

The Service may generate or collect:

- audit events (login events, permission changes, exports, administrative actions),
- query and workflow metadata (such as time of query, feature used, and high-level query parameters),
- API logs (endpoint called, timestamps, response codes, rate limit events),
- operational telemetry (latency, reliability metrics, diagnostics, and security signals).

The Service is designed for investigations and analytics. Logs and telemetry are used to secure and improve performance. BitInt does not aim to collect more personal data than needed for those purposes.

### 3.4 Customer Data and investigation content

Customers may upload or generate content inside the Service. Depending on Customer configurations and workflows, **Customer Data** may include:

- blockchain identifiers (addresses, transaction IDs, scripts, outputs, and related identifiers),
- Customer-created labels, watchlists, and risk tags,
- case notes, attachments, and investigation narratives,
- IP addresses, device identifiers, or other security indicators included in incident response context,
- contact details and counterpart information that a Customer enters into a case.

BitInt does not require Customers to submit personal data about natural persons for the Service to function. Customers should avoid uploading unnecessary personal data and should follow internal data minimization policies.

### 3.5 Communications and support

BitInt may process:

- support tickets and communications,
- call recordings or meeting notes where applicable and where legally permitted,
- troubleshooting artifacts a Customer shares, such as screenshots or logs,
- correspondence related to procurement, onboarding, renewals, and legal requests.

### 3.6 Billing and payment data

BitInt may process billing contact details, invoicing data, and subscription information. Payment processing is often handled through third-party payment providers. BitInt aims to avoid storing full payment details where not necessary.

## 4. Sources of information

BitInt may collect information from:

- individuals directly (such as form submissions and support messages),
- Customer administrators (such as provisioning of Authorized Users),
- integrations enabled by Customer (such as SSO providers and security tooling),

## 5. How we use information

BitInt uses personal data for purposes aligned with providing, securing, and improving a B2B SaaS platform. Purposes may include:

- providing and operating the Service, including authentication, authorization, and account administration,
- securing the Service, including fraud prevention, abuse detection, access monitoring, and incident response,
- delivering support, troubleshooting issues, and responding to requests,
- managing billing, subscriptions, and renewals,
- improving product performance, reliability, and user experience through aggregated analytics,
- meeting legal obligations and responding to lawful requests.

Where marketing communications occur, BitInt aims to use professional, opt-in or opt-out compliance practices appropriate to jurisdiction and context.

## 6. Legal bases for processing (GDPR and UK GDPR)

For individuals in the EEA and UK, BitInt relies on one or more of the following legal bases, depending on context:

- **Contract** - to provide the Service and perform obligations under an agreement,
- **Legitimate interests** - to secure the Service, prevent abuse, improve reliability, and operate a B2B SaaS platform,
- **Consent** - for certain cookies and similar technologies where required, and for certain marketing preferences,
- **Legal obligation** - to comply with applicable laws, lawful orders, and regulatory requirements.

Where BitInt acts as a processor, Customer determines the legal basis and BitInt processes personal data on documented instructions.

## 7. How we share information

BitInt may share personal data in limited circumstances:

- **Service providers and subprocessors** used for hosting, infrastructure, analytics, customer support tooling, email delivery, and payment processing,
- **Professional advisors** such as lawyers, auditors, and insurers, subject to confidentiality obligations,
- **Legal and compliance disclosures** where required by law or where BitInt responds to binding requests,
- **Corporate transactions** such as mergers, acquisitions, or asset transfers, subject to appropriate safeguards.

BitInt does not publish Customer investigation content. Sharing of Customer Data is driven by Customer account permissions and Customer export actions.

## 9. Data retention

- Customer Data retention is governed by Customer instructions, service configuration, and any applicable DPA.
- Support communications may be retained to improve support quality, maintain continuity, and meet compliance obligations.

Public blockchain data is not deleted from the blockchain, since BitInt does not control that infrastructure. Section 15 covers this nuance.

No system is perfectly secure. BitInt cannot guarantee that security incidents will never occur.

## 15. Blockchain data nuance

Blockchain intelligence has unique characteristics.

- **On-chain data is public.** Bitcoin and other public blockchains are designed to be publicly verifiable. Anyone can observe transactions and related identifiers.
- **On-chain data is immutable.** BitInt cannot remove, edit, or delete data written to a blockchain.
- **BitInt does not control blockchains.** The Service reads and analyzes on-chain data, but it does not operate or administer the networks.
- **Customer annotations are different.** Customer-created labels, case notes, attachments, and internal watchlists are stored within the Service and are subject to Customer retention instructions and service configuration.

Risk scores, labels, and entity assessments are analytic opinions derived from data analysis. They are not statements of fact about a natural person.

## 16. Automated analysis and decisioning

The Service performs automated analysis to generate Outputs such as traces, risk scores, and clustering assessments. Automated analysis supports investigations and compliance workflows.

BitInt does not make legally significant decisions on behalf of Customers. Customers control how they use Outputs and should apply human review and corroboration appropriate to the risk and the context.

Where automated decisioning rules apply under certain laws, Customer accounts may require additional contractual terms or configuration controls.

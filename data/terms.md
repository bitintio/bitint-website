## 11. Outputs, limitations, and no reliance

### 11.1 Probabilistic analytics

Outputs reflect analytic assessments and heuristic inferences. Errors can occur due to data ambiguity, incomplete context, evolving adversary behavior, and limitations inherent in the underlying sources.

False positives can occur when unrelated activity appears linked. False negatives can occur when related activity is obscured.

### 11.2 Clustering and attribution limitations

Clustering heuristics may be affected by factors such as:

- CoinJoin and other collaborative transaction constructions,
- Taproot and script-path behaviors that change visibility of spending conditions,
- shared infrastructure such as hosted wallets, exchanges, and custodial services,
- address reuse, change handling, and wallet software idiosyncrasies.

Entity attribution and labels represent assessments, not statements of fact about a natural person.

### 11.3 Network-level observations

Where the Service offers network-level or P2P-layer insights, such insights are inherently noisy and depend on network topology, reachability, timing, and measurement limitations. Outputs should be treated as investigative leads and should be corroborated.

### 11.4 No reliance for sole decision-making

Customer agrees that Outputs will not be the sole basis for legally significant decisions. Customer will apply human review and corroboration appropriate to the risk.
